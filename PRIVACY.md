# Privacy Policy

**Last updated:** March 2026

## What is Hagbits?

Hagbits is an anonymous lifestyle quiz app for teens. It asks 16 questions across sleep, screen time, diet, and physical activity, then returns a personalised result. No account is required.

## What data we collect

When you complete the quiz, your answers are stored alongside a randomly generated identifier (UUID). We do **not** collect:

- Your name
- Your email address
- Your IP address
- Device identifiers or cookies for tracking

The only stored data is your quiz answers (as numeric scores) and the UUID that makes your results link work. There is no way to link this back to you as an individual.

## How your data is used

Quiz responses are used solely to generate and display your results page. Anonymised, aggregated response data may be used to improve questions and scoring — no individual record is ever singled out.

## Data storage

Data is stored in [Firebase Firestore](https://firebase.google.com/products/firestore) (Google Cloud), a SOC 2 certified NoSQL document database. Data is stored in the US by default. We do not share data with third-party analytics or advertising services.

## Children's privacy

Hagbits is intended for users aged **13 and above**. Users under 13 should have a parent or guardian's permission before using the app. Because no personal information is collected, the privacy risk is minimal regardless of age.

## Cookies

Hagbits does not use cookies for tracking or analytics. Quiz state is held in browser memory and is cleared when you close the tab.

## Your rights

Because responses are fully anonymous, we cannot retrieve or delete a specific person's data on request. If you have a concern, please [open an issue on GitHub](https://github.com/hagmandan/hagbits/issues).

## Changes to this policy

This policy will be updated as new features (such as profiles or habit tracking) are introduced. The "last updated" date at the top reflects the most recent revision.

## Contact

Questions? Open an issue at [github.com/hagmandan/hagbits](https://github.com/hagmandan/hagbits/issues).
